During the first half of the 20th century, much of the world was at war.

Because war is so resource-intensive, many countries asked their citizens to pitch in and contribute. Even our presidents did their part. In 1918, President Woodrow Wilson and First Lady Edith Wilson brought sheep to graze and fertilize the White House lawns in an effort to save manpower, fuel, and chemicals, so they could instead be used in the war. In 1943, the Roosevelts planted a "victory garden" on the White House grounds. A victory garden was a private or community vegetable garden, either in a backyard or public area, where people could grow food for their families and communities. This was done in order to reduce demands on the country’s resources.
